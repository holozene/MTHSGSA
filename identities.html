Gender Identities



Agender: a person that has no gender and feels no connection to any genders,
regardless of their assigned gender at birth or previous gender identities.



Bigender: A person whose gender identity falls between two genders.
They either have two gender identities simultaneously or switch between the two.



Demigender: a person that has a weak or partial connection to a certain gender
(demigirl, demiboy, demiandrogyne)



Genderfluid: a person that identifies as male, female, or nonbinary
at different times or circumstances.



Genderqueer: A gender variant person whose gender identity is neither male nor
female, is between or beyond genders, or is some combination of genders, similar
to non-binary.



Non-binary: a spectrum of gender identies that are not exclusively masculine
or feminine - identities that are outside of the gender binary, similar to
genderqueer.



Pangender: A person whose gender identity is comprised of all or many gender expressions.



Transgender people have a gender identity or gender expression that differs
from their assigned sex. Some transgender people desire medical assistance
to transition from one sex to another. Transgender – often shortened as trans
– is also an umbrella term: in addition to including people whose gender
identity is the opposite of their assigned sex (trans men and trans women),
it also includes people who are not exclusively masculine or feminine (people
who are non-binary or genderqueer, including bigender, pangender, genderfluid,
or agender).

Cisgender: describes someone who feels comfortable with the gender identity and gender
expression expectations assigned to them based on their physical sex.

Sexual and Romantic Orientations



Asexual: Someone being asexual means that they don't experience sexual attraction,
or that they experience a limited amount. Asexual can be a stand-alone identity,
or it can be used as an umbrella term.



Aromantic: Someone being aromantic means that they don't experience romantic attraction,
or that they experience a limited amount. Aromantic can be a stand-alone identity,
or it can be used as an umbrella term.



Bisexual/romantic: A person emotionally, physically, romantically, and/or sexually attracted
to multiple genders. This attraction does not have to be equally split between genders and
there may be a preference for one gender over others.



Demisexual/romantic: a person who doesn't feel sexual/romantic attraction towards another
person until they form an emotional connection with them. This orientation is commonly
associated with the ace/aro spectrum.



Lesbian: Term used to describe female-identified people attracted romantically, sexually,
and/or emotionally to other female-identified people. The term lesbian is derived from
the name of the Greek island of Lesbos.



Pansexual/romantic: A person who is sexually/romantically attracted to all or many gender
expressions.



Homosexual/romantic / Gay: A person emotionally, physically, romantically,
and/or sexually attracted to members of the same gender.

Heterosexual/romantic / Straight: A person emotionally, physically, romantically, and/or
sexually attracted to the "opposite" gender from their own.
